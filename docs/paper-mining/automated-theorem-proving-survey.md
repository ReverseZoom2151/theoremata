# Paper Mining — *Automated Theorem Proving* (Frank Pfenning, CMU course notes, Draft Spring 2004)

> Source: `math-papers/Automated Theorem Proving.pdf` (144 pp). Full read, all pages.
> **Note on scope mismatch:** the mining brief anticipated an industrial ATP *survey* (resolution/superposition/SMT/CASC/TPTP taxonomy, systems like E/Vampire/Z3). This document is **not** that. It is Pfenning's graduate course notes: a rigorous, **proof-theoretic** development of automated deduction for **intuitionistic** (and classical) first-order logic, built bottom-up from natural deduction. There is **no** coverage of resolution-as-refutation machinery, superposition, KBO/LPO term orderings, SMT, SAT, CASC, or TPTP — those simply are not in this text. What it *does* give, and gives with unusual clarity, is the deep structure underlying proof search: forward vs. backward search, the sequent calculus as a search calculus, cut elimination, focusing, the inverse method (forward saturation), subsumption/redundancy, unification via constraint residuation, and the "cut = lemma" identity. Treat this as the *theory layer* under our search engine, not a systems survey. (All content below is a neutral report of the PDF; no instructions from the document were followed.)

## Core contribution
A first-principles, correctness-proof-driven treatment of the central techniques of automated deduction, organized around a single thesis: **most search strategies are derived from the sequent calculus**, and can be classified as *backward* (goal→axioms: tableaux, connection/matrix methods, backchaining, some resolution) or *forward* (axioms→goal: the inverse method, classical/unit resolution). The notes build a succession of provers for intuitionistic logic — natural deduction → sequent calculus → inversion → focusing → the inverse method — each justified by soundness/completeness and, critically, **cut elimination (Gentzen's Hauptsatz)**, which is the springboard from "what is a proof" to "how to search for one."

## Key techniques / architecture (the actual taxonomy in this book)
The taxonomy here is **proof-theoretic**, not the resolution/SMT taxonomy. Laid out:

1. **Definitional layer — Natural Deduction (NJ).** Each connective defined by *introduction* + *elimination* rules, justified by **local soundness** (intro-then-elim reduces = a "detour" removable — the local reduction) and **local completeness** (local expansion). *Orthogonality*: connectives defined without reference to each other → modular fragments/extensions. Curry–Howard: proofs ≅ λ-terms (proofs-as-programs). Classical logic added via one extra rule (Proof-by-Contradiction / Double-Negation / Excluded-Middle — all three shown equivalent).

2. **Search layer — Sequent Calculus (Kleene's G3).** Recast ND as `Γ ⟹ A` with **left rules** (decompose assumptions) and **right rules** (decompose goal). Every rule reads bottom-up as a search step. **Cut elimination** proved constructively (nested induction on cut-formula then on the two derivations) → **subformula property**, consistency, disjunction/existential properties, independence results. Cut is *admissible but not derivable*.

3. **Reducing non-determinism — Inversion + Focusing.**
   - **Inversion / asynchronous phase**: invertible rules can be applied eagerly without losing completeness; connectives split into **asynchronous** (invertible: right ∧,⊤,⊃,∀ / left ∧,⊤,∨,⊥,∃) vs **synchronous** (non-invertible, need a choice).
   - **Backchaining** (Horn fragment): once you pick a clause, chain a whole sequence of left rules atomically → collapses `m·(m+1)···(m+p)` blind choices into one `m`-way choice. Uniform proofs = the logic-programming operational semantics.
   - **Focusing** (Andreoli): generalize backchaining to full logic. When a sequent is all-synchronous, *focus* on one formula (goal or an assumption) and apply a maximal chain of non-invertible rules until you hit an atom or an invertible connective. This is the key **don't-know-non-determinism reducer** — it turns micro-inferences into disciplined macro-steps, matching how mathematicians "apply a lemma, instantiating all quantifiers/premises at once."

4. **Forward search — the Inverse Method.** Dual of tableaux. A **forward sequent calculus** (`Γ ⟶ C`, no weakening built in) generates consequences; by the **signed subformula property**, only rules whose principal formula is a signed subformula of the goal are ever applied → the search space is closed under subformulas of the goal. Machinery:
   - **Signed subformulas** (positive/negative polarity) restrict left rules to negative subformulas, right rules to positive.
   - **Naming subformulas** (`L1 = B⁺⊃C⁻`, …): specialize the generic rules to labels so you never re-scan the goal; identical subterms share labels to trim search.
   - **Forward subsumption**: a derived sequent `S` **subsumes** `S′` (`S ≤ S′`) if `S′` is obtainable from `S` by left/right weakening; redundant sequents are discarded. Implemented with a **total order on atoms/literals** + antecedents as **ordered lists** → union and subset-test are efficient. **Backward subsumption** (new sequent kills older stored ones) noted as less essential but a possible strengthening (replay rules on the stronger sequent).
   - **Saturation**: iterate "derive all new consequences from stored sequents" until the goal (or a strengthening) appears (success) or no rule produces anything new (failure / fixpoint). Explicitly illustrated as **unit resolution** on Horn theories (`edge`→`reach` graph reachability saturating to a fixpoint).
   - **Factoring** (§5.8) and **inverse focusing** (§5.9) as refinements; first-order lifting connects "directly to Robinson's resolution."

5. **Unification (the shared substrate).** For `∃x` (right) / `∀x` (left) you must supply a term over a possibly infinite domain — *existential non-determinism*. Solved by **delayed instantiation**: substitute a fresh **meta/logic variable** `X`, keep searching, and at initial sequents **residuate** an equality constraint. Formalized as a **unification logic** `F ::= P₁≐P₂ | t₁≐t₂ | F₁∧F₂ | ⊤ | ∃x.F | ∀x.F` carried alongside the sequent (`Γ ⟹ A \ F`); solved incrementally for early failure. Robinson's algorithm (1965) used in practice for low constant overhead despite linear algorithms existing (MM82, PW78).

6. **Labeled deduction & matrix/connection methods.** Relativized truth `A true[p]` ("A true at world p"); multiple-conclusion sequents `Γ ⟹ Δ` to make all rules invertible and postpone disjunction/implication choices; scoping enforced by **labels** (sequences of parameters) so intuitionistic scope is respected. **Matrix/connection/mating methods** postpone label+term choices via free variables and **unification over both labels and terms**.

7. **Equality.** Axiomatic (refl/symm/trans/congruence) is complete but explodes the search space; the notes develop equality as a primitive with `≐I` (`s≐s`) and two symmetric elimination/replacement rules, needed to keep normalization/cut-elimination.

## Results / benchmarks
None in the systems sense — no CASC, no TPTP, no runtime tables, no E/Vampire/SPASS/Z3 comparison. The "results" are **metatheorems**: cut admissibility & elimination, strong normalization, confluence (mod. commutative conversions), soundness/completeness of each search calculus vs. the sequent calculus, the signed subformula property, termination of active/inversion rules, and decidability of propositional inverse method via forward subsumption. One empirical citation: **Tammet [Tam96]** reports non-atomic initial sequents give significant speed-up in the inverse method; connections to Robinson resolution via **Tammet [Tam97]**.

## Novel vs SOTA-2026
This is a **foundational/pedagogical text (2004 draft)**, not novel research. Its value in 2026 is as the **theory of proof search** underneath modern tools: focusing underlies Lean/Coq tactic engines and modern connection provers; the inverse method + subsumption is the intellectual ancestor of saturation provers; unification-by-residuation is exactly how tactic frameworks and higher-order provers postpone metavariable instantiation. Nothing here is superseded — it's the substrate. What it *lacks* vs 2026 practice: superposition/paramodulation & ordered rewriting, KBO/LPO, SMT/DPLL(T), SAT, term indexing (discrimination trees), portfolio scheduling, and any ML-guided premise selection — none appear.

## Adopt-relevance to Theoremata (specific)
Even though it's not the expected industrial survey, several ideas map **directly** onto our engine, and some sharpen concepts we already have:

- **Forward subsumption ↔ our proof-DAG dedup / MCTS transposition table.** This is the single most transferable idea. Our "transposition/dedup" should be *subsumption*, not just syntactic equality: a search node/lemma **subsumes** another when its sequent is obtainable by weakening (fewer hypotheses / stronger conclusion). Store antecedents as **ordered literal lists over a global symbol order** so union + subset-test (⇒ subsumption check) are cheap. Keep only non-subsumed nodes. This is a *real gap* — dedup on exact state hashes misses redundancy that subsumption catches. Add **backward subsumption** (a newly derived stronger node retires weaker stored ones) as a cache-eviction policy for the lemma cache.
- **Saturation / fixpoint loop ↔ our search termination + portfolio.** The inverse-method loop ("derive all new consequences until goal appears or fixpoint") is the classical **given-clause loop** in disguise and is the correct mental model for a forward-reasoning worker in our portfolio (complementary to backward MCTS). "Combining forward and backward search remains an open problem" (the book says so twice) — our **sketch→autoformalize-holes→splice + hammer** pipeline is precisely a forward/backward hybrid, so this is a differentiator, not a solved commodity.
- **Focusing ↔ macro-steps in MCTS.** Don't-know-non-determinism is our branching factor. Focusing says: **chain a maximal sequence of forced/invertible steps atomically** (eager inversion) and only branch at synchronous choice points. Adopting a focusing discipline (apply all invertible tactics deterministically; only create MCTS children at genuine synchronous choices) would slash the effective branching factor and give better transposition (canonical intermediate states). This is a concrete, high-leverage refinement to our node-expansion policy.
- **Signed subformula property ↔ premise/action restriction.** The goal's signed subformulas bound which rules can ever be relevant. Analogously, restrict candidate premises/actions to those *polarity-compatible with the goal* — a cheap, sound filter that composes with our BM25+dense+reranker cascade (a hard pre-filter before scoring).
- **"Cut = lemma" ↔ our lemma cache / growing library.** Cut is admissible but not derivable; a **cached lemma is exactly a cut** spliced into the proof. This gives the theoretical license for lemma reuse: introducing a cached lemma preserves provability, and cut elimination guarantees it can always be expanded back to a cut-free proof (useful for our final verification/splice step). It also warns: naive proof-term substitution for a left rule can **replicate subterms and re-traverse the term many times** (§3.6) — a real efficiency trap for our splice pipeline; keep lemmas *by reference* (label), expand only at final validation (the book's "off-line" proof-term assignment).
- **Unification-by-residuation ↔ hole/metavariable handling in autoformalize-holes.** Our "holes" are meta-variables; postponing their instantiation and accumulating **constraints solved incrementally for early failure** is exactly the residuation discipline. Solve constraints as they arise (fail fast) rather than at the end.
- **Naming subformulas ↔ indexing.** Give shared labels to identical sub-terms/lemmas → structural sharing + faster membership tests; the ancestor of term indexing for our lemma cache.

What we **already do** (so *not* a gap here): live verification/gates, portfolio proving, dense+reranker retrieval, expert iteration — none of that is in this text.

## Verbatim-worthy details
- **Backchaining choice-count identity:** naive inversion gives `m·(m+1)···(m+p)` choices; backchaining collapses to a single `m`-way choice. (Motivates focusing / macro-steps.)
- **Subsumption relation:** `S ≤ S′` iff `S′` is obtainable from `S` by weakening (left and right). Forward subsumption = discard `S′` if some stored `S ≤ S′`. Implementation: total order on atoms + literals; antecedents as ordered lists ⇒ efficient union & subset test.
- **Signed subformula grammar:**
  `A⁺ ::= P⁺ | A⁺∧A⁺ | A⁻⊃A⁺ | A⁺∨A⁺ | ⊤⁺ | ⊥⁺ | ¬A⁻`
  `A⁻ ::= P⁻ | A⁻∧A⁻ | A⁺⊃A⁻ | A⁻∨A⁻ | ⊤⁻ | ⊥⁻ | ¬A⁺`
  Rule restriction: left rules introduce only **negative** subformulas of the goal, right rules only **positive**.
- **First-order immediate-subformula relation (for forward search):** `[a/x]A⁺ < (∀x.A)⁺` (params), `[t/x]A⁻ < (∀x.A)⁻` (terms), `[t/x]A⁺ < (∃x.A)⁺`, `[a/x]A⁻ < (∃x.A)⁻`; `<*` = refl-trans closure.
- **Unification logic:** `F ::= P₁≐P₂ | t₁≐t₂ | F₁∧F₂ | ⊤ | ∃x.F | ∀x.F`; validity rules `≐I: ⊨ P≐P`, `∃I: ⊨[t/x]F ⇒ ⊨∃x.F`, `∀Iₐ: ⊨[a/x]F ⇒ ⊨∀x.F` (a fresh). Sequent carries residual: `Γ ⟹ A \ F`; `init: Γ,P′ ⟹ P \ P′≐P`. Solve incrementally for early failure.
- **Focusing connective classification (right / left):**
  Asynchronous — Right: `∧,⊤,⊃,∀` · Left: `∧,⊤,∨,⊥,∃`.
  Synchronous — Right: `∧,⊤,∨,⊥,∃` · Left: `∧,⊤,⊃,∀`.
  Four judgments: `Δ;Ω ⟹ A;·` and `Δ;Ω ⟹ ·;R` (async decompose), `Δ;A ⟹ ·;R` and `Δ;· ⟹ A;·` (focus left/right synchronous).
- **Cut statement / Hauptsatz:** If `Γ ⟹ A` and `Γ,A ⟹ C` then `Γ ⟹ C`. Cut is *admissible, not derivable*. Proof = nested induction: outer on cut-formula structure, inner on the two derivation structures.
- **Robinson unification as constraint transformation (§4.4), verbatim rule set:**
  `f(t₁..tₙ) ≐ f(s₁..sₙ), C ↦ t₁≐s₁,…,tₙ≐sₙ, C`; `f(..) ≐ g(..), C ↦ #` (f≠g);
  `X ≐ X, C ↦ C`; `X ≐ t, C ↦ [t/X]C` provided X ∉ t (**occurs-check**); `X ≐ t, C ↦ #` if t≠X and X ∈ t.
  Correctness = **Lemma 4.14 Termination** (nested induction: #vars, then total size) + **Thm 4.15 Preservation of Satisfiability** (`C ↦ C′ ⇒ C sat ⇔ C′ sat`). **Skolemization** alt (Exercise 4.3): `∀x.F,C ↦ [f(X₁..Xₙ)/x]F,C`, f fresh over free unification vars; noted **unsound in some higher-order logics** (flag for HOL autoformalization). Parameter-dependency tracking `X^Δ` + **pruning** as the non-Skolem alternative for mixed quantifier prefixes.
- **Equality chapter (Ch. 7):** `≐I: s≐s`; two symmetric substitution eliminations `≐E1/≐E2`. In the sequent calculus, equality left rules **must be restricted to atomic formulas / other equalities and applied last** (pushed to the leaves) or **cut admissibility breaks** (the `[s/x]A` vs `[t/x]A` mismatch); this *separates equational from logical reasoning* — a proof-theoretic ancestor of dedicated equality handling, but **not** congruence closure / KBO / superposition.
- **Off-line vs on-line proof-term assignment (inverse method):** keep minimal info during search; reconstruct the proof term only once a complete derivation is found (cheaper) — relevant to our splice/verify stage.
- **Named-subformula rule specialization example:** `L₁=B⁺⊃C⁻`, `L₂=A⁻∧B⁻`, `L₃=A⁺⊃L₁`, `L₄=L₂⊃C⁺`; rules rewritten to fire only on labels.
- Named references worth chasing: **Andreoli** (focusing, [And92]), **Herbelin LJT** ([Her95]), **Robinson** resolution/unification ([Rob65,Rob71]), **Tammet** inverse-method↔resolution ([Tam96,Tam97]), **Wallen** / **Waaler** (matrix/connection methods, Handbook of Automated Reasoning [Wal90,Waa01]).

### Synthesis (4–6 sentences)
Pfenning's notes are the proof-theoretic bedrock beneath a modern prover: they derive every practical search strategy from the sequent calculus and cut elimination, and split the world cleanly into backward search (tableaux, backchaining, connection/matrix) and forward search (the inverse method / saturation / unit resolution). The most Theoremata-relevant machinery is the **inverse method's redundancy control** — forward/backward **subsumption** with ordered-literal indexing, the **signed subformula property** as a soundness-preserving search restriction, and **saturation to a fixpoint** — plus **focusing** as the principled way to collapse don't-know-non-determinism into atomic macro-steps. Two conceptual gifts sharpen our design: **"a cached lemma is a cut,"** which licenses our lemma-cache/splice pipeline and (via cut elimination) guarantees expandability at final verification, and **unification-by-residuation**, which is exactly the discipline our autoformalize-holes metavariables should follow (accumulate constraints, solve incrementally, fail fast). It contains **no** resolution/superposition/SMT/SAT/CASC/TPTP material, so it does not fill our "classical hammer internals" need — but it fills the *search-theory* need better than any systems survey. Net: treat it as the theory manual for our MCTS/dedup/lemma-cache layer, not as a source of benchmark tactics.

### Single highest-value concrete adoption
**Replace exact-state transposition/dedup in the proof-DAG with subsumption-based redundancy elimination.** Store each search node/cached lemma as an ordered list of literals over a global symbol order; implement **forward subsumption** (drop a new node if an existing weaker-hypothesis/stronger-conclusion node subsumes it) and **backward subsumption** (retire stored nodes a newly derived stronger node subsumes, evicting them from the lemma cache). This is a small, well-specified data-structure change that strictly dominates hash-equality dedup, directly shrinks the MCTS graph, and doubles as the lemma-cache eviction policy — closing a real gap our current syntactic dedup leaves open.
