# Towards Autonomous Mathematics Research (Aletheia)

Feng, Trinh, Bingham, … Le, Luong et al., Google DeepMind, 2026-02-11 (arXiv). Project page: github.com/google-deepmind/superhuman/tree/main/aletheia.

## Core contribution
Introduces **Aletheia**, a natural-language math *research* agent (not formal/Lean) built on an advanced Gemini Deep Think, that iteratively **generates → verifies → revises** solutions end-to-end. Demonstrates real research milestones: a fully AI-generated paper (eigenweights, "Level A" autonomy), human-AI collaborations, and a semi-autonomous sweep of 700 open Erdős problems yielding 4 autonomous resolutions. Proposes governance artifacts: a two-axis **Autonomous Mathematics Levels** taxonomy (autonomy × significance) and **Human-AI Interaction (HAI) Cards** for transparency.

## Key techniques / architecture (the research-agent loop)
- **Three subagents orchestrated in a loop**: **Generator** (produces candidate solution), **Verifier** (natural-language, checks correctness), **Reviser** (fixes flaws). They "interact continuously until a solution is found that the Verifier approves, or attempts reach a preset hyperparameter limit." Each subagent internally orchestrates multiple calls to a Gemini base model.
- **Core design insight**: **decouple the model's final output from its intermediate thinking tokens**, then add prompt scaffolding — this lets the model recognize flaws it overlooked during generation. Hypotheses: RL training incentivizes guessing/bluffing; long thinking traces act as misleading "supporting" context inflating a wrong answer's probability. Takeaway: **explicitly separating the verification step is effective in practice** — this is the whole basis of the agent, and it directly motivates our falsify-before-prove / meta-verification gate.
- **Informal verifier as a filter/gate**: on Erdős sweep, the verifier reduced 700 prompts → 212 "potentially correct" candidates for human review. Crucially, "Aletheia often admits failure to solve a problem" — the verifier makes the agent *abstain* rather than bluff, which improved human-AI collaboration efficiency. On IMO-Proof-Bench it returned solutions for 29/30 problems (conditional accuracy 98.3%); on PhD-level FutureMath Basic it answered <60% with >82% conditional accuracy. Abstention + high conditional accuracy is the reliability lever.
- **Inference-time scaling law**: Deep Think uses *parallel thinking* (explore many ideas at once), so compute is tunable; accuracy rises orders of magnitude with compute before plateauing, and the scaling transfers from Olympiad to PhD level. New Jan-2026 base model reached IMO-Proof-Bench parity at ~100× less compute. Conclusion: scaling alone insufficient for research → need the agentic harness.
- **Intensive tool use**: Google Search + web browsing. Without tools the model hallucinates spurious citations (fabricated titles/authors); training for tool use eliminates obvious fabrications but shifts errors to subtler ones (real reference, misquoted result). Python-as-tool gave only marginal gains on computational hallucinations.
- **Conjecture generation / research workflow (from HAI cards)**: notably the human-AI workflow was sometimes *reversed* — the AI supplied the high-level strategy ("use these dual sets", log-convexity, reduction techniques, key lemmas) and humans filled in rigorous execution. The BKKKZ26 card shows an explicit **generalize-a-solved-problem loop**: solve base problem → "state and prove a meaningful, interesting generalization" → weaken hypotheses (lim=∞ → limsup=∞) → prove rigorously → find optimality examples.

## Results / benchmarks
- **IMO-Proof-Bench Advanced (30 problems)**: Aletheia 95.1% overall (vs prior best 65.7% Gemini Deep Think IMO-Gold, Jul 2025); 98.3% conditional accuracy on the 29 it answered.
- **FutureMath Basic (PhD-level)**: SOTA; answered <60%, >82% conditional accuracy.
- **Erdős sweep (700 open problems, Dec 2025)**: verifier surfaced 212 → experts graded. Of 200 markable candidates: 137 (68.5%) fundamentally flawed, 63 (31.5%) technically correct, only **13 (6.5%) meaningfully correct**. 13 clustered into: Autonomous Resolution (652, 1051), Partial (654, 1040), Independent Rediscovery (397, 659, 935, 1089), Literature Identification (333, 591, 705, 992, 1105). 4 "autonomous" resolutions claimed.
- **Ablation**: plain Gemini Deep Think (IMO scale, same base model, ~2× compute) solved 8/13 of Aletheia's Erdős problems; reproduced FYZ26 & BKKKZ26 & ACGKMP26 but failed Feng26 (all 3 prompts) and the crucial LeeSeo26 prompt — i.e., the agentic harness matters for the hardest cases.
- Research papers: Feng26 (eigenweights, fully AI content), LeeSeo26 (independence polynomials), BKKKZ26 (generalized Erdős-1051), ACGKMP26 (robust MDP bounds via Siegel's Lemma), FYZ26.

## Novel vs SOTA-2026
State-of-the-art 2026 result for *natural-language* autonomous research (contrast AlphaProof/AlphaGeometry which use formal language). The durable, adoptable ideas: (1) generate/verify/revise with an **informal NL verifier that enables abstention**; (2) **thinking-token decoupling** to surface self-errors; (3) the **autonomy×significance taxonomy + HAI cards** as governance/eval scaffolding; (4) honest failure analysis (specification-gaming: the model misinterprets ambiguous problems toward the easiest answer; "subconscious plagiarism" from pretraining).

## Adopt-relevance to Theoremata — vs our agentic loop + blueprint-run + conjecturing
- **We already do**: generate→verify→revise orchestration, meta-verification/critique, portfolio proving, proof-DAG. Aletheia validates our core architecture at frontier scale.
- **Real gaps / adopt**:
  1. **Abstention as a first-class output.** The single biggest reliability lever here is the verifier making the agent *decline* rather than bluff (98.3% conditional accuracy comes from answering only when confident). Our loop should expose an explicit "insufficient confidence / cannot verify" terminal state and report *conditional* accuracy, not just coverage. High value.
  2. **Thinking-token decoupling.** Have the Verifier/Reviser operate on the *final claimed proof only*, stripped of the Generator's chain-of-thought, to avoid the CoT inflating a wrong answer. Cheap prompt-architecture change to our critique step.
  3. **NL informal verifier alongside the formal 3+1 gate.** Aletheia shows formal verification "cannot even formulate the questions of interest on most research frontiers," so an NL verifier is needed where autoformalization fails — this is the same complementary NL/formal split as the ProofGrader paper. Our meta-verification should include an NL-verifier arm gated for research-scale statements that resist autoformalization.
  4. **Autonomy×significance taxonomy + HAI cards** → adopt as metadata schema on our blueprint/paper-scale runs: tag each result with (autonomy level H/C/A, significance 0–4) and emit a machine-readable interaction card (raw prompts+outputs for essential AI insights). Fits our provenance/proof-DAG and expert-iteration audit trail.
  5. **Conjecturing loop = "solve → generalize → weaken hypotheses → prove → find optimality examples."** Concrete recipe for our conjecture-generation module; pairs naturally with falsify-before-prove (the "find examples demonstrating results are optimal" step is exactly counterexample search).
  6. **Tool-hallucination lesson for our retrieval**: web/search access reduces fabricated citations but not subtle misquotes — our premise/literature retrieval needs a *citation-faithfulness* check (does the cited source actually state the claimed result?), not just retrieval.
  7. **Failure taxonomy for eval**: report the 4-way Erdős breakdown analog (fundamentally-flawed / technically-correct / meaningfully-correct / ambiguous) and beware specification-gaming on ambiguous statements — feeds our ProofGrader + benchmark design.

## Verbatim-worthy details
- Agent = Generator + Verifier + Reviser subagents, loop "until the Verifier approves, or attempts reach a preset (hyperparameter) limit"; each subagent orchestrates internal Gemini base-model calls.
- Design principle: "decoupling a reasoning model's final output from its intermediate thinking tokens, and adding well-chosen prompt scaffolding, enables the model to recognize flaws it initially overlooked."
- IMO-Proof-Bench Advanced: 95.1% (29/30 answered, 98.3% conditional); FutureMath Basic <60% answered, >82% conditional. New base model ≈100× compute reduction for equal IMO-Proof-Bench performance.
- Erdős: 700 open → 212 candidate → 200 markable: 137 (68.5%) fundamentally flawed / 63 (31.5%) technically correct / 13 (6.5%) meaningfully correct / 12 ambiguous. Ablation: Deep Think 8/13 at ~2× compute.
- **Autonomy levels**: H = Primarily Human (AI minor/auxiliary); C = Human-AI Collaboration (both essential); A = Essentially Autonomous (core content fully AI, humans accountable for final paper).
- **Significance levels**: 0 negligible (Olympiad/PhD-exercise) · 1 minor novelty · 2 publication grade · 3 major advance (top-5 journals) · 4 landmark breakthrough. Their results span A0–C2/A2; "public should not read C2/A2 as AI reaching human-mathematician level."
- HAI Card format = alternating Human query / AI(model) response rows, capturing essential insights, with link to raw prompts+outputs. Baseline transparency norm for Level C/A: expose the most important raw prompts/outputs containing essential AI-generated insights.
- Failure modes: spurious-citation hallucination (fabricated then misquoted references); specification gaming / reward hacking (misinterpret ambiguous problems toward easiest answer); "subconscious plagiarism" (reproducing pretraining knowledge without attribution). Authorship principle: papers human-authored because "authorship implies accountability … only humans can bear," incl. attribution accuracy that "cannot be guaranteed by formal verification."
