## 0. TL;DR for Theoremata

DeepSeek-Math-V2 is a **natural-language** (not formal) theorem-proving system whose entire thesis is the one thing our harness is missing: a **trained, faithful proof verifier used as the reward signal**, plus a **generator that self-verifies and refines until it can no longer find an issue**. The open-source release is **weights + prompts + a test-time inference/refinement harness only** — no training code, no verifier weights separate from the generator, no datasets. The gold is (a) the paper's RL recipe (verifier → meta-verifier → generator, all GRPO) and (b) the released `inference/main.py` test-time loop, which is a **drop-in blueprint for our `critic` + `reason` retry/plan-history layers** and directly generalizes our "falsify-before-prove" gate into a scored, iterated generate→verify→meta-verify→refine pool.

## 1. What it is (scope, size, core method, results)

### Scope & size
Tiny repo — it is a *release artifact*, not a codebase. Full inventory:

- `README.md` — model card; built on **DeepSeek-V3.2-Exp-Base**; weights on HF `deepseek-ai/DeepSeek-Math-V2`; Apache-2.0 (`LICENSE`).
- `inference/` — the entire runnable surface (~970 LOC Python):
  - `main.py` (523 LOC) — the multi-round **generate → verify → meta-verify → refine** orchestration loop.
  - `generate.py` (181 LOC) — async OpenAI-compatible client + multiprocessing batch runner with resumable checkpointing (pickled `complete_batches`).
  - `math_templates.py` (213 LOC) — the **4 prompt templates** (proof_generation, proof_verification, meta_verification, proof_refinement). These are the real IP in the repo.
  - `utils.py` (50 LOC) — `extract_boxed_answers`, `extract_solution`, `extract_self_eval` parsing helpers.
  - `run.sh` — example invocation.
- `inputs/` — eval problem sets as JSON: `IMO2025.json`, `CMO2024.json`, `CMO2025.json`, `Putnam2024.json`. Schema: `{id, question, answer, contest, problem_idx}`.
- `outputs/` — model predictions (`.jsonl`) for IMO2025, CMO2024, Putnam2024, **IMO-ProofBench-Basic/Advanced**. Schema: `{question, problem_idx, model_prediction:{proof, ...}}`; ProofBench rows add `{solution, "grading guidelines", level, source, type}`.
- `figures/` — result plots.

**No training code, no reward-model weights, no RL datasets, no MCTS.** Everything about training in §2 lives only in the PDF prose.

### Core method (PDF pp. 2–6)
Three models trained in an **iterative loop**, all with **GRPO** (Shao et al. 2024, DeepSeekMath v1's algorithm), starting from DeepSeek-V3.2-Exp-Base:

1. **Verifier** `π_φ(·|X,Y,I_v)` (pp. 2–3): given problem `X` + proof `Y` + rubric `I_v`, emits an issue-summary then a score ∈ {0, 0.5, 1} (1 = complete/rigorous, 0.5 = minor gaps, 0 = fatal). Trained by GRPO on cold-start data `D_v={(X,Y,s)}` (17,503 AoPS olympiad problems `D_p`; proofs generated by a V3.2 variant with iterative refinement; expert-scored). Reward = `R_format · R_score`, where `R_score = 1 − |s'−s|` (Eq. 1–2, p. 3).

2. **Meta-verifier** `π_η(·|X,Y,V,I_mv)` (pp. 3–4): audits a verifier analysis `V` — checks whether the issues it *claims* actually exist and justify the score. This fixes the key exploit: a verifier can score a flawed proof correctly *while hallucinating fake issues*. Enhanced verifier reward becomes **`R_V = R_format · R_score · R_meta`** (Eq. 3). Reported effect: verifier analysis quality 0.85 → **0.96** with no loss of scoring accuracy (p. 4).

3. **Generator** `π_θ(·|X)` (pp. 4–5): trained with the verifier as a **generative reward model**, objective `max E[R_Y]` (Eq. 4). Crucially it is trained to emit **proof `Y` + self-analysis `Z`** in the verifier's own format; reward blends both:
   `R = R_format(Y,Z) · (α·R_Y + β·R_Z)`, `R_Z = R_score(s',s) · R_meta(Z)`, with **α=0.76, β=0.24** (Eq. 5–6, p. 5). This makes the model "explicitly aware of its reward function" and incentivizes finding+fixing its own issues before finalizing.

4. **Synergy / self-improving data flywheel** (pp. 5–6): stronger generator → harder proofs → challenge the verifier → those become new verifier training data. **Auto-labeling** replaces human annotation: for each proof generate `n` verifications; for issue-reporting ones generate `m` meta-verifications (majority confirms validity); label proof with the lowest score confirmed by ≥`k` valid analyses, else label 1. In the last two iterations this **fully replaced human labeling** (p. 6). Training order per iteration: optimize verifier first, init generator from verifier checkpoint, then from iteration 2 the verifier is re-init from a rejection-fine-tuned checkpoint consolidating both skills (p. 6, §3.1).

### Results (pp. 6–9)
- **IMO 2025: 5/6 solved, 83.3%** — gold. **CMO 2024: 4/6 + partial, 73.8%** — gold. **Putnam 2024: 118/120** (11/12 fully), beating the top human score of 90 (Table 1, p. 8).
- **IMO-ProofBench**: beats DeepMind DeepThink (IMO-Gold) on Basic, competitive on Advanced; far above GPT-5 / Gemini-2.5-Pro / Grok-4 / DeepSeek-R1 (Fig. 3, p. 8).
- **Sequential self-verification refinement** (Fig. 2, p. 7): on ISL 2024, Pass@1 rises monotonically 0.15→0.27 as max iterations go 1→8; Best@32 (self-selected) 0.26→0.42 — self-selected-best ≫ thread-average, proving the model's self-scoring is discriminative.
- One-shot: beats GPT-5-Thinking-High and Gemini-2.5-Pro across all 5 CNML categories (Fig. 1, p. 6).

## 2. Reusable ideas for Theoremata — THE priority

Mapping each to our components (`reason` = proof/plan generation, `train` = STaR/GRPO scaffolds, `critic` = falsify/self-grade, `verify` = multi-formal-system verification).

### 2.1 Trained faithful verifier as the reward model → `critic` + `train`
**Idea (pp. 2–4).** Don't just prompt a critic; *train* a verifier to emit `{issue summary, score∈{0,0.5,1}}` and use its score as the RL reward for the generator. The scoring rubric is fully released (`math_templates.py` `proof_verification`, PDF App. A.2 p. 14):
> "- If the solution is completely correct … score is 1 / - generally correct but minor errors … 0.5 / - does not address the problem, fatal errors, severe omissions … 0. Additionally, referencing anything from any paper does not save the need to prove the reference…"

**Map to us.** Our `critic`/falsify-before-prove currently gates with heuristics + prompted checks. Adopt this **three-valued {0, 0.5, 1} scored rubric as the taint/critic contract** — note it aligns naturally with our existing **three-valued taint** (valid / suspect / refuted ≈ 1 / 0.5 / 0). Wire the score as the scalar reward in our `train` GRPO/STaR scaffold. `R_score = 1 − |s'−s|` (Eq. 1) is the exact reward shaping to copy.

### 2.2 Meta-verification (verify the verifier) → `critic` self-audit, anti-hallucination
**Idea (pp. 3–4, App. A.3).** A second model grades the *analysis*, not the proof: does each claimed issue actually exist, and does it justify the score? Reward multiplies in `R_meta` (Eq. 3). Directly attacks critic hallucination — the failure mode where a critic invents flaws to look rigorous. The full meta-rubric is released (`math_templates.py` `meta_verification`): it forces **defect analysis** (does the flaw exist + is the description accurate), **step-restatement checking**, **expression analysis**, and **score analysis**, and notably instructs the meta-verifier to *only* audit claimed defects (positive claims are out of scope).

**Map to us.** This is the highest-leverage *new* concept for our `critic` layer. Add a **meta-critic pass** over any critic/falsifier output before it's allowed to reject a node in the proof-DAG — prevents false-negatives from killing valid blueprint nodes. Multiplicatively gate: a rejection only counts if a meta-check confirms it (mirrors the auto-label "majority of meta-assessments confirm" rule, p. 6).

### 2.3 Generator with in-line self-verification + faithful self-score → `reason`
**Idea (pp. 4–5, App. A.1).** The generator is trained to output `## Solution` then `## Self Evaluation` ending in `\boxed{0|0.5|1}`, and is rewarded for *faithful* self-assessment (via `R_Z = R_score·R_meta`), not for claiming correctness. Prompt is explicit and quotable (App. A.1 p. 13):
> "you already have the ability to rate your solution yourself … Only when you fail to locate any issues should you score it with 1 … The worst final response would provide a wrong solution but lie that it's correct … You CAN'T cheat! If you cheat, we will know, and you will be penalized!"

Key finding (p. 4): a generator asked to self-grade in one shot **over-claims correctness** unless separately trained/rewarded for honest self-eval — motivates the dedicated `R_Z` term. `α=0.76,β=0.24` weighting (p. 5) keeps proof-correctness dominant but honesty non-trivial.

**Map to us.** Our `reason` node output schema should carry a **self-eval block + self-score** as a first-class field, parsed exactly like `utils.py::extract_self_eval` / `extract_boxed_answers`. QED-style retry should consume the self-score as the stop condition (see 2.4). This is the mechanism that makes "falsify-before-prove" *internal* to generation rather than a separate stage.

### 2.4 Test-time scaling loops — DIRECTLY RUNNABLE → `reason` retry + plan-history memory
This is the released code and maps 1:1 onto our retry/plan-history/MCTS ambitions. Two loops:

**(a) Sequential refinement** (PDF §3.3.2 p. 7; prompt App. A.4). Generate proof+self-eval → if self-score < 1, re-prompt with prior output via `proof_refinement` template → repeat until self-score = 1 or max iters (they use up to 8). Stop condition = self-assigned perfect score. **This is our QED retry loop with a principled termination signal.**

**(b) High-compute pooled search** (PDF §3.3.3 p. 8; `inference/main.py`). Per problem maintain a **proof pool** persisted on disk (`proof_pool/{source}/{problem_idx}.jsonl`, each record `{proof, meanscore, score2ratings, self_eval, proof_id, dep_proof_ids, round_idx}`). Each round:
  1. Generate 64 proofs, 64 verifications each (`generate.py`).
  2. `prepare_proof_verification` (main.py:66) splits out proof + self-eval, builds verification prompts.
  3. `prepare_meta_verification` (main.py:118) — only meta-verifies analyses with **score ≤ 0.75** (skips confident-pass cases; `if score > 0.75: continue`, main.py:133).
  4. `prepare_proof_refinement` / `_prepare_proof_agg_tasks` (main.py:165–284): rank pool by `(meanscore, self_eval_score)`, take top-`n_best_proofs_to_sample` (32), form combinations of `n_proofs_to_refine` proofs, pair each with up to 8 ratings (prioritizing those with issues, `max_rating_per_score`), feed the `proof_refinement` template. Records **`dep_proof_ids`** so each refined proof tracks its provenance — **a literal proof-DAG / plan-history lineage**.
  5. Stop when a proof passes all 64 verifications (`meanscore > 0.99999`, main.py:222) or 16 rounds.

**Map to us.** `_prepare_proof_agg_tasks` is essentially a hand-rolled MCTS-lite / beam over a scored candidate pool with provenance edges — port its **pool schema, `dep_proof_ids` lineage, and score-ranked refinement selection** into our plan-history memory and MCTS. The `meanscore > 0.99999` all-pass gate and the "meta-verify only low-scoring analyses to save compute" trick are cheap, concrete wins. The resumable checkpoint pattern in `generate.py` (pickle `complete_batches`) is worth copying for our long benchmark runs.

### 2.5 Auto-labeling flywheel → `train` data engine
**Idea (p. 6).** Generate proofs → verify `n×` → meta-verify `m×` → label by majority-confirmed lowest score, requiring ≥`k` valid analyses; else label 1 or route to humans. Fully replaced human annotation in the final two iterations.

**Map to us.** A concrete recipe for **bootstrapping labeled proof-correctness data with zero human labels** for our STaR/GRPO scaffolds — using our own multi-formal-system `verify` (Lean/Rocq/Isabelle compile results) as an *even stronger* ground-truth signal than DSM-V2's LLM verifier. We can hybridize: formal-verification pass = hard label 1; NL verifier + meta-verifier for the (majority of) statements that resist autoformalization.

### 2.6 GRPO recipe specifics → `train`
- Algorithm: **GRPO** throughout (p. 6). Reward structures: verifier `R_format·R_score` (Eq. 2); enhanced verifier `·R_meta` (Eq. 3); generator `R_format·(α·R_Y+β·R_Z)` (Eq. 5–6).
- **Format reward** = indicator that output contains the required marker phrases ("Here is my evaluation of the solution:" and a `\boxed{}` score after "Based on my evaluation, the final overall score should be:") — p. 3. Cheap, exact, copyable into our reward fn.
- Iteration/init discipline (§3.1 p. 6): verifier-first, generator init from verifier, later verifier re-init from rejection-FT consolidated checkpoint. A clean curriculum for our alternating train loop.

## 5. New vs. what we already built

**New / not yet in Theoremata (adopt):**
- **Meta-verification (verify-the-verifier)** — we have no critic-of-the-critic; this is the standout new idea and pairs perfectly with our three-valued taint.
- **Trained verifier as GRPO reward** with `R_score = 1−|s'−s|` and the {0,0.5,1} rubric — concretizes what our `train` scaffolds should optimize.
- **Faithful self-eval reward split** (`α·R_Y + β·R_Z`, honesty-rewarded) — a training signal our STaR/GRPO scaffold lacks.
- **Scored proof-pool with `dep_proof_ids` provenance + rank-and-refine selection** — a ready-made enrichment of our plan-history memory / MCTS with lineage edges.
- **Zero-human auto-labeling flywheel** (n verify × m meta-verify, majority-confirm) — a data engine for `train`.
- Cheap engineering wins: format-reward marker phrases, "meta-verify only score ≤ 0.75", all-pass `>0.99999` stop gate, resumable batch checkpointing.

**Overlaps / we already do it (and often go further):**
- **Falsify-before-prove** ≈ their generator self-verification + issue-finding, but ours is a *separate DAG gate*; theirs is *trained into generation*. Consider merging: keep our gate but add their in-line self-score as the retry stop signal.
- **QED-style retry + plan-history** ≈ their sequential refinement + proof pool — same shape, we can borrow their exact selection/termination logic.
- **MCTS** — they don't use MCTS; their pooled combinatorial refinement is a simpler alternative worth benchmarking against our MCTS.
- **Multi-formal-system live verification (Lean/Rocq/Isabelle + hammers + premise retrieval)** — **entirely absent here; this is our moat.** DSM-V2 is deliberately *informal* NL-only (they cite Lean/Isabelle/DeepSeek-Prover-V2/Seed-Prover in Related Work, p. 9, and explicitly say NL advances will "benefit formal reasoning"). Strongest synergy: **use our formal `verify` as the hard-label oracle inside their auto-labeling flywheel**, giving a stronger reward than their LLM verifier for the autoformalizable subset.
- **LiteLLM model-agnostic provider** — their `generate.py` is a thin single-provider `AsyncOpenAI` client; ours is more capable. No adoption needed.

**Net:** take the *training-time* ideas (verifier/meta-verifier/self-eval GRPO recipe) and the *test-time* pool/refinement schema; ignore the inference plumbing. Our formal-verification stack is complementary and, plugged into their flywheel, a genuine improvement over their all-LLM labeling.
